# Rule corrections: current law versus the BoatSafe Workbook 6th edition

The workbook and factsheets this app was researched from predate several
law changes. Question content MUST follow this file wherever it differs
from the knowledge base. Verified against the in-force Transport
Operations (Marine Safety) Regulation 2016 (TOMSR) on legislation.qld.gov.au
and MSQ marine information bulletins, July 2026.

## Lifejacket wear rules (commenced 1 December 2024, fines from 1 July 2025)

Current TOMSR s24 requires a lifejacket to be WORN:

1. Open boat under 4.8 m, underway, daytime, with only one person aged 12
   or older aboard (boating alone, or alone with children under 12): that
   person must wear one, and every child aged 1 to under 12 must wear one.
2. Open boat under 4.8 m, underway at night (sunset to sunrise): everyone
   aged 1 year or older must wear one.
3. Open boat of ANY length, underway: every child aged 1 to under 12 must
   wear one. (The old rule limited this to boats under 4.8 m.)
4. A ship that is not an open boat, underway: every child aged 1 to under
   12 occupying an open area (outside the cabin or deckhouse) must wear one.
5. Crossing a designated coastal bar (TOMSR Schedule 1): everyone aged 1+
   on an open boat of any length; on other ships, everyone aged 1+ in an
   open area. A separate gazette notice extends bar-crossing wear to small
   sailboats, pedal boats, rafts, rowboats, and sea or surf kayaks.

Definitions: an "open boat" has no permanent rigid deckhouse, cabin or
enclosed occupiable space. "Underway" means not anchored, moored or made
fast to the shore (electric spot-lock or GPS-hold counts as underway).
Infants under 1 year are outside the wear laws; MSQ recommends they not
travel on recreational boats.

Penalty enforcement began 1 July 2025 with fines starting at $333.

## Lifejacket standards (from 1 September 2025)

Only AS 4758 lifejackets (levels 50S, 50, 100, 150, 275) satisfy carriage
and wear requirements. The old PFD Type 1 / Type 2 / Type 3 jackets made
to AS 1512, AS 1499 or AS 2260 are no longer acceptable. Any workbook
reference to "PFD Type 1/2/3" is outdated terminology.

Levels required by water (TOMSR s22, unchanged):
- Boat, smooth waters: level 50S, 50, 100 (or compliant inflatable diving jacket).
- Boat, partially smooth waters: level 50 or 100 (50S not acceptable).
- Boat, beyond partially smooth waters: level 100 or higher only.
- PWC: level 50 or 50S in smooth waters; level 50 elsewhere. Level 100
  collared jackets must NOT be used on a PWC.

## Level flotation exemption removed (1 June 2024)

Old TOMSR s13(2) let a boat with grab rails and an approved level
flotation statement skip carrying lifejackets in rivers, creeks and
protected waters. That exemption was deleted. Lifejackets must be carried
for every person aged 1 and over on all boats in all waters (and worn in
the situations above).

## Lake Wivenhoe marine zone (December 2024)

A new marine zone the workbook does not mention: no PWC operation, engine
restrictions (electric, 4-stroke, or direct-injection 2-stroke only),
6-knot speed limit, no operation between sunset and sunrise, no towing,
and a prohibited area near the dam wall.

## Assessment facts (as run in 2026)

- The knowledge test is the Common Assessment Tool (CAT): closed-book
  multiple choice, run face to face by BoatSafe providers. RMDL is
  typically 50 questions; the PWC add-on is typically 20 more; a
  standalone PWCL assessment is typically 40.
- The pass requirement is effectively 100 percent: wrong answers trigger
  retraining and a same-day retest on the missed questions (maximum two
  attempts per day).
- Time limit 45 minutes. Theory must be passed before the practical.
- Minimum age 16 for both licences; the BoatSafe statement of competency
  expires after 6 months; a PWCL is only issued to RMDL holders.
- Licences are delivered through the Queensland Digital Licence app and
  never expire.

## Confirmed unchanged (safe to teach from the workbook)

0.05 alcohol limit for the person in control; boat 6-knot rule within
30 m of people, anchored or moored ships, jetties, ramps and pontoons;
PWC 6-knot rule within 60 m of those plus the shore and bathing reserves;
PWC 10-knot rule within 30 m of another moving ship; PWC freestyling ban
within 200 m of residential shoreline; EPIRB 406 MHz required beyond
smooth waters or more than 2 nm from land; registration threshold 3 kW;
licence threshold over 4.5 kW; V sheet plus 2 red hand flares plus 2
orange smoke signals beyond smooth waters; flares 3-year life.
